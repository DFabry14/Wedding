FROM FRIENDS TO FABRYS

We actually don't remember meeting each other. Although we both grew up in Park Ridge & were in the same graduating class at Maine South High School, we didn't meet until summer of 2009. We were both home after graduating college & spent the summer hanging out with mutual friends. Specifically, every Wednesday, at Duffy's Bar with The Spazmatics, $1 Bud Lites, & $4 bombs.


Our friendship grew deep, fast - we talked every single day since we met. I spent the summer as an unpaid intern at FEMA & Dan spent the summer prepping to play professional volleyball overseas. I still work for FEMA (they pay me now) & Dan spent 4 seasons playing professional volleyball in Sweden & London (where he got his Masters Degree).


When Dan returned home for good, we started dating. On September 11, 2015, to be exact! The rest is history...



THE PROPOSAL

Dan had the ring for a few months, but the timing just wasn't working out.


I was deployed on disaster duty for a month, came home for one week, & was going back on deployment for another month. Dan took that one week as his opportunity to change our lives.


We took Norman for a walk along the lake. I stepped in goose poop & would not stop complaining about it. We sat on the lake-front steps where he told me that almost 4-years ago, to the date, we went on one of our coolest dates right here - the Adler Planetarium. He then got down on one-knee & asked me to marry him!


The ring took my breath away, but the answer was obviously YES!


We spent some more time along the lake with Norman, enjoying the view...of both my ring & the city. We asked our parents to meet us for dinner so we could tell them in person, at the same time. In the meantime, we stopped at Portillo's so Dan could finally get something to eat, now that he wasn't nervous!

Change Of Plans

Due to the COVID-19 pandemic, we are cancelling the big, beautiful wedding we had planned for July 25, 2020.


We will still be getting married this summer but we have decided for the health & safety of everyone to only have our immediate family present at our nuptials.


Although we know we are making the right decision, we are very much grieving the biggest celebration of our lives.


Thank you for your love & support. Whether you've offered to walk Norman on our wedding day, planned parties for us, sent encouraging messages, or cried with us while video chatting... we will be forever grateful for you.


We can't wait to celebrate (something, anything!) with you soon. Until then, stay safe, stay sane, stay healthy.
